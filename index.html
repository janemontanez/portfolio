About

I am a JD and MLIS candidate at UCLA preparing for a career as a law librarian. My legal training was completed at the University of San Francisco School of Law (2023), with two years in the Immigration Policy Clinic researching and drafting Ninth Circuit appeals and advocating on San Francisco police-code reforms for non-native English speakers. My MLIS work at UCLA has been built deliberately around the law-librarianship track — academic libraries, description and access, user experience, and data management — and grounded in an internship at the Law Library of Congress improving the metadata for congress.gov historical bill summaries.

I am targeting academic law libraries and firm-based research and knowledge services, and I contribute to the profession through the UCLA Committee on Library and Scholarly Communication (COLASC) and as Director of Internal Affairs for the Information Studies Graduate Student Association.

Tails From the Metaverse ▼ – A multimodal insider ethnography of the furry community in VRChat (n=1,000). Call for participants open.

Call for Participants

I'm a graduate student and community member looking for 1,000 participants to share their stories as part of the largest qualitative study of the furry community ever conducted.

What we'll talk about:
  • Your fursona and what it means to you
  • How you experience identity in VRChat
  • Your thoughts on AI and AI-generated art
  • Geopolitics, economics, and daily life in virtual space
What participation looks like:
  • A casual, open-ended conversation in VRChat
  • Screen-recorded with your informed consent
  • You choose how you're identified
  • You review and approve anything before publication

Outputs: A peer-reviewed academic paper, a transcription archive, and a video series in the tradition of Joe Hunting's VRChat documentaries.
