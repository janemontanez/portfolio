Major Paper from an Elective

Polling DeepSeek-R1: A Digital Humanities Analysis of LLM Censorship and the U.S.–China AI Arms Race Narrative

INF STD 298B: Digital Humanities as Methodology of Critique-Creation · Winter 2025 · Grade: A

Abstract

This digital humanities project systematically polls DeepSeek-R1 with one hundred ChatGPT-generated questions on topics sensitive to the Chinese government, captures R1's refusals and sentiment, and analyzes the resulting corpus using the NLTK VADER sentiment model. Grounded in Chela Sandoval's methodology of the oppressed and in feminist data practices (Hill Collins, Haraway), the paper reads LLM censorship as a political artifact rather than a technical one. It intervenes in the dominant U.S.–China “AI arms race” narrative and demonstrates how model-level content controls encode state positions through measurable patterns of rejection and affective flattening.

Context & Reflection

INF STD 298B was a PhD-level digital humanities seminar on critique-creation methodology. The course emphasized that DH work is not neutral computation but a political and positional practice. I chose DeepSeek-R1 as the object because its public release in early 2025 was being framed, in the U.S., primarily through arms-race rhetoric. I wanted to test whether a methodology of the oppressed — locating my own situated labor rather than hiding behind toolchain “objectivity” — could produce a more honest account of what the model actually does when asked about state-sensitive topics.

The paper is representative of the work I expect to do as a law librarian: research-heavy, grounded in reproducible methodology (the Python scripts and CSVs are published alongside the paper), honest about positionality, and committed to making the infrastructure of its own claims auditable by other readers. The combination of descriptive work (polling, sentiment coding) with critical reading (what the pattern of rejection actually encodes) is how I have approached legal research throughout my career — and it is how I intend to approach reference, collection, and bibliographic instruction in a law library.
